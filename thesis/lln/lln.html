Law of Large Numbers

In probability theory the law of large numbers is a theorem that describes the results of an experiment performed a large number of times. It states that: the average of the results obtained from a large number of trials should be close to the expected value and tends to become closer to the expected value as more trials are performed

There are two forms of this law.

Weak form

The weak law of large numbers states that the sample average converges in probability towards the expected value.
